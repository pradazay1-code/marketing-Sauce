# Sales Framework SOP

## When to Use
Invoked whenever the user is preparing for a sales call, needs a sales script, is following up with a prospect, or needs help closing a deal.

## Sales Philosophy
- Sell by solving problems, not by pitching services
- Ask questions more than you talk
- The prospect should be doing 60-70% of the talking
- Focus on their pain, then show how you remove it
- Never be pushy — be genuinely helpful

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Pre-Call Research
Before any sales interaction:
- [ ] Review lead info from `memory/leads.md`
- [ ] Visit their website and social media (fresh look)
- [ ] Note 2-3 specific problems you can solve for them
- [ ] Check if they've engaged with any outreach previously
- [ ] Prepare 3-5 discovery questions specific to their business
- [ ] Know your offer inside and out (pricing, deliverables, timeline)

### Step 2: The Sales Call Structure

**Opening (2-3 minutes)**
- Thank them for their time
- Brief intro (who you are, what you do — 30 seconds max)
- Set the agenda: "I'd love to learn about your business, see if there's a fit, and if not, no worries at all."
- Ask permission to ask questions

**Discovery (10-15 minutes)**
Key questions to ask:
1. "Tell me about your business — what do you do and who do you serve?"
2. "How are you currently getting new customers/clients?"
3. "What's working well for you right now marketing-wise?"
4. "What's your biggest frustration with your current marketing?"
5. "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about your marketing, what would it be?"
6. "What have you tried in the past that didn't work?"
7. "What does your ideal outcome look like 6 months from now?"
8. "What's holding you back from getting there?"

Listen for pain points. Write them down. Repeat them back.

**Transition (1-2 minutes)**
- Summarize what you heard: "So if I'm hearing you correctly..."
- Bridge to your solution: "This is exactly what we help businesses like yours with."

**Presentation (5-7 minutes)**
- Address their SPECIFIC pain points with your services
- Use case studies or results from similar clients
- Keep it simple — 3 main things you'll do for them
- Show, don't just tell (examples, before/afters, numbers)

**Pricing (2-3 minutes)**
- Present pricing confidently
- Tie the price to the value/ROI they'll get
- Offer 2-3 options if possible (good/better/best)
- Be transparent — no hidden fees

**Close (2-3 minutes)**
- Ask: "Based on what we've discussed, does this sound like something that would help your business?"
- Handle objections (see framework below)
- If yes: outline next steps (contract, onboarding call, first deliverable)
- If not now: set a follow-up date, stay in touch

### Step 3: Objection Handling

**"It's too expensive"**
→ "I totally understand. Let me ask — if this brings you [X results], would that be worth the investment? Let's talk about what ROI looks like for businesses like yours."

**"I need to think about it"**
→ "Of course, take your time. What specific things do you want to think through? I want to make sure you have all the info you need."

**"I already have someone doing this"**
→ "That's great you're already investing in marketing. Just curious — are you happy with the results you're getting? What would you change if you could?"

**"I've been burned before"**
→ "I hear that a lot, honestly. What happened? ... That makes sense. Here's how we're different: [specific differentiator]. And we have a [guarantee/trial/proof] to back it up."

**"Now's not a good time"**
→ "I get it. When would be a better time to revisit? I'll set a reminder and follow up then. In the meantime, I'll send you [something valuable — case study, tips, audit]."

### Step 4: Post-Call Follow-Up
Within 24 hours of the call:
- [ ] Send thank-you email with recap of discussion
- [ ] Include any materials promised (case studies, proposals)
- [ ] Set follow-up reminder in pipeline
- [ ] Update lead status in `memory/leads.md`
- [ ] Log call notes and outcome in `memory/session-log.md`

### Step 5: Proposal Creation (If Needed)
If the prospect needs a formal proposal:
- Use template from `templates/`
- Customize with their specific pain points and proposed solutions
- Include: scope of work, timeline, deliverables, pricing, terms
- Send within 24-48 hours of the call

## Quality Checks
- [ ] Pre-call research is thorough
- [ ] Discovery questions are open-ended and specific to them
- [ ] Presentation addresses THEIR problems (not a generic pitch)
- [ ] Objections handled with empathy, not aggression
- [ ] Follow-up sent within 24 hours
- [ ] Lead status updated in pipeline
