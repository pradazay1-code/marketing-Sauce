# Client Outreach SOP

## When to Use
Invoked whenever the user asks to reach out to leads, send emails, draft messages, or contact potential clients.

## Philosophy
Every outreach message must feel like it was written by a human who actually researched the business. No templates that feel like templates. Personalization is everything.

## Tools Required
- Lead data from `memory/leads.md`
- Outreach templates from `templates/`
- Email drafting capability
- Social media messaging (LinkedIn, Instagram DM frameworks)

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Select Leads
- Pull leads from `memory/leads.md` that are marked "New Lead" or "Ready for Outreach"
- Prioritize by lead score (highest first)
- Confirm batch size with user (default: 10 per session)

### Step 2: Research Each Lead (Deep Dive)
For each lead, before writing anything:
- Visit their website — note specific issues (slow load, bad design, missing pages)
- Check their social media — note follower count, posting frequency, engagement
- Look at their Google reviews — note themes in customer feedback
- Check if they're running ads — note quality and targeting issues
- Find something personal — recent award, new location, community involvement

### Step 3: Choose Outreach Channel
Based on the lead, select the best channel:
- **Cold Email** — Best for most B2B outreach
- **LinkedIn DM** — Best for professional services, B2B
- **Instagram DM** — Best for local businesses, restaurants, retail
- **Phone Call Prep** — For high-value leads, prepare a call script
- **Multi-channel** — For top leads, prepare messages for 2-3 channels

### Step 4: Write Personalized Message
Structure for every outreach message:
1. **Hook** (1 sentence) — Reference something specific about THEIR business
2. **Observation** (1-2 sentences) — What you noticed about their marketing
3. **Value** (1-2 sentences) — What you could do for them (specific, not vague)
4. **Social Proof** (1 sentence) — Brief result or credibility marker
5. **CTA** (1 sentence) — Simple, low-commitment ask

### Step 5: Review and Stage
- Save all drafted messages
- Present to user for review before sending
- User approves, edits, or rejects each message
- Mark approved messages as "Ready to Send"

### Step 6: Follow-Up Sequence
If no response after initial outreach:
- **Day 3:** Short follow-up referencing original message
- **Day 7:** New angle — share a free tip or observation
- **Day 14:** Break-up email — "No worries if timing isn't right"
- Mark lead as "Followed Up" or "No Response" accordingly

### Step 7: Log Results
- Update `memory/leads.md` with outreach status
- Track: sent date, channel, response received (Y/N), outcome
- Log what messaging worked/didn't in `memory/learnings.md`

## Message Templates

### Cold Email Template
```
Subject: Quick thought on [Business Name]'s [specific thing]

Hey [First Name],

I was checking out [Business Name] and noticed [specific observation about their marketing].

We help [industry] businesses [specific benefit] — recently helped a similar [industry] business [specific result].

Would you be open to a quick 10-minute chat this week? No pressure at all.

[Your Name]
```

### LinkedIn DM Template
```
Hey [First Name] — saw [something specific about them or their business]. Really cool what you're building with [Business Name].

Quick question — have you thought about [specific marketing opportunity you spotted]? I work with [industry] businesses on exactly this.

Happy to share a couple quick ideas if you're interested. No pitch, just value.
```

### Instagram DM Template
```
Hey! Love what you're doing with [Business Name] 🔥

Noticed [specific observation]. Have you tried [specific suggestion]? Helped a similar business [result].

Would love to chat if you're open to it!
```

## Quality Checks
- [ ] Every message references something SPECIFIC about the business (not generic)
- [ ] No message is longer than 100 words (for email, slightly longer is OK)
- [ ] CTA is clear and low-commitment
- [ ] Tone matches the channel (professional for LinkedIn, casual for Instagram)
- [ ] No spelling or grammar errors
- [ ] Follow-up sequence is personalized, not just "checking in"

## Notes
- Best outreach times: Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11 AM or 2-4 PM local time
- Response rates to track: aim for 10-20% reply rate on cold outreach
- Always A/B test subject lines and hooks
- Log winning messages in `memory/learnings.md` for future reference
