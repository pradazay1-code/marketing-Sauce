# Content Creation SOP

## When to Use
Invoked whenever the user asks to create social media content, write copy, develop content calendars, create graphics direction, or produce any marketing content for clients or the agency.

## Content Types
- Social media posts (Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, TikTok, X/Twitter)
- Blog articles and SEO content
- Email marketing (newsletters, sequences, campaigns)
- Video scripts (short-form and long-form)
- Ad copy (coordinate with ad-manager agent)
- Website copy (coordinate with website-builder agent)
- Brand messaging and taglines

## Tools Required
- Copywriting frameworks
- Content calendar template from `templates/`
- Brand voice guidelines from client files
- Hashtag and keyword research
- Content scheduling knowledge
- Image/video direction guides

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Content Strategy
Gather from user or `memory/clients.md`:
- [ ] Brand voice and tone (professional, casual, edgy, friendly, etc.)
- [ ] Target audience
- [ ] Content goals (brand awareness, engagement, leads, sales)
- [ ] Platforms to post on
- [ ] Posting frequency
- [ ] Content pillars (3-5 recurring themes)
- [ ] Competitor content analysis

### Step 2: Define Content Pillars
Every brand should have 3-5 pillars they rotate through:
1. **Educational** — Teach something valuable to the audience
2. **Behind-the-Scenes** — Show the human side of the business
3. **Social Proof** — Testimonials, results, case studies
4. **Promotional** — Offers, services, CTAs
5. **Engagement** — Questions, polls, interactive content

### Step 3: Create Content Calendar
- Plan 2-4 weeks of content at a time
- Map content to pillars (ensure variety)
- Align with any upcoming events, holidays, or promotions
- Include platform-specific adaptations
- Save calendar to `templates/content-calendar-template.md`

### Step 4: Write Content
For each piece of content:

**Social Media Post Structure:**
1. **Hook** (Line 1) — Stop the scroll. Question, bold statement, or pattern interrupt
2. **Body** (2-5 lines) — Deliver value, tell the story, share the insight
3. **CTA** (Last line) — Tell them what to do next
4. **Hashtags** — 5-15 relevant hashtags (research per platform)

**Blog Article Structure:**
1. **Headline** — Clear, benefit-driven, SEO-optimized
2. **Intro** — Hook + promise of what they'll learn
3. **Body** — Subheadings, short paragraphs, bullet points
4. **Conclusion** — Summary + CTA
5. **SEO** — Keywords in title, headers, meta description, alt tags

**Email Structure:**
1. **Subject Line** — Create curiosity or state a clear benefit
2. **Preview Text** — Complement the subject line
3. **Body** — One main idea, conversational tone, clear CTA
4. **P.S.** — Secondary CTA or urgency element

### Step 5: Review and Approve
- Present content to user for review
- Make revisions based on feedback
- Get final approval before scheduling/posting
- Save approved content for future reference

### Step 6: Performance Tracking
After posting, track:
- Engagement rate (likes, comments, shares, saves)
- Reach and impressions
- Click-through rate (if applicable)
- Follower growth
- Best performing content types and topics
- Log insights in `memory/learnings.md`

## Content Voice Guidelines

### For the Agency's Own Content:
- Confident but not arrogant
- Results-focused
- Conversational — write like you talk
- Use specific numbers and results when possible
- Short sentences. Short paragraphs.

### For Client Content:
- Match the client's existing voice (or develop one with them)
- Stay consistent across all platforms
- Document voice guidelines in client file

## Quality Checks
- [ ] Content matches the brand voice
- [ ] No spelling or grammar errors
- [ ] Hook is strong enough to stop the scroll
- [ ] CTA is clear
- [ ] Hashtags are relevant and researched
- [ ] Image/video direction is specific enough to create
- [ ] Content is original (not copied from competitors)
- [ ] Complies with platform guidelines

## Notes
- Save high-performing content formulas to `memory/learnings.md`
- Repurpose content across platforms (adjust format, not recreate)
- Track trends monthly and adapt content pillars accordingly
- Build a swipe file of great content inspiration in `templates/`
